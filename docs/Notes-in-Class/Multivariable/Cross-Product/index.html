Cross Product

Original Definition​

We know that given two vectors u\mathbf{u}u and v\mathbf{v}v

u=u1i+u2j+u3k\mathbf{u} =u_1\mathbf{i}+u_2\mathbf{j}+u_3\mathbf{k}u=u1​i+u2​j+u3​k v=v1i+v2j+v3k\mathbf{v} =v_1\mathbf{i}+v_2\mathbf{j}+v_3\mathbf{k}v=v1​i+v2​j+v3​k

The cross product u×v\mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{v}u×v is defined as a vector that is orthogonal to the plane created by u\mathbf{u}u and v\mathbf{v}v.

The magnitude:
∥u×v∥=∥u∥∥v∥sin⁡θ\|\mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{v}\|=\|\mathbf{u}\|\|\mathbf{v}\|\sin{\theta}∥u×v∥=∥u∥∥v∥sinθ

Related to Parallelogram​

It is obvious that according to our originial definition of cross product, the magnitude of the cross product is also equal to the area of parallelogram created by u\mathbf{u}u and v\mathbf{v}v

Related to parallelogram:
∥u×v∥=∥u∥∥v∥sin⁡θ=Sparallelogram\|\mathbf{u}\times \mathbf{v}\|=\|\mathbf{u}\|\|\mathbf{v}\|\sin{\theta}=S_{\text{parallelogram}}∥u×v∥=∥u∥∥v∥sinθ=Sparallelogram​
